## Interpretation Rules

- **Artifact transfer** means the same frozen harness, skill, memory, guidance, or controller is reused on another model.
- **Method reuse** means the algorithm is rerun on each target model and may produce target-specific artifacts.
- **Atomic** means one declared harness factor changes while task, model, environment, and primary budget are held fixed.
- **Held-out target** means the target model did not contribute intervention outcomes to fitting or selection. Merely hiding target tasks is insufficient.
- **Prospective effect prediction** means a signed or numerical intervention effect is committed before target intervention outcomes are observed.

## What the Matrix Supports

1. **Positive transfer is real.** Life-Harness, CoEvoSkills, Trace2Skill, HarnessFix, and Meta-Harness make a universal-negative thesis untenable.
2. **Negative transfer is also real.** SEAGym, Probe-and-Refine, MemDelta, SkillCraft, and the skill-lifecycle matrix show sign or efficiency reversals.
3. **The unit of transfer is usually bundled.** Most direct papers transfer whole workspaces, multi-layer repairs, strategy programs, or skill packages.
4. **Atomic heterogeneity is now occupied.** The CCI paper and MemDelta prevent claiming novelty from one-factor ablations alone.
5. **Held-out prediction is occupied for absolute performance and policy selection.** PACE, Agent Psychometrics, and cost-aware skill rewriting prevent a generic "first predictor" claim.
6. **The unoccupied conjunction is narrower:** target-model-conditioned prediction of the sign and magnitude of an atomic harness intervention on prospectively sealed model-task cells, evaluated as a deploy/abstain decision under matched budgets. SEAGym's H-FS explanation should be tested directly through failure-surface-nearest and opportunity-only baselines, not avoided.

## Current Confidence

- Confidence that "transfer is heterogeneous" is already established: **high**.
- Confidence that atomic harness effects can change sign across models/tasks: **high for prompt/memory components, medium for executable runtime controls**.
- Confidence that no paper in this corpus prospectively predicts atomic target effects on a sealed new model: **medium-high**, not final. Search must continue because the frontier is moving daily.
