## Author Abstract

Large language models are increasingly deployed as autonomous agents for multi-step workflows in real-world software environments, but existing benchmarks are limited by trajectory-opaque grading, underspecified safety and robustness evaluation, and narrow coverage. We introduce Claw-Eval, an end-to-end suite with 300 human-verified tasks across general service orchestration, multimodal interaction, and multi-turn professional dialogue. Each run is recorded through execution traces, audit logs, and environment snapshots, yielding 2,159 rubric items. The protocol evaluates completion, safety, and robustness across three trials. Experiments on 14 frontier models show that trajectory-opaque evaluation misses many safety and robustness failures, capability does not imply consistency, and model rankings vary across task groups and metrics.
