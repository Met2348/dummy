## Author Abstract

When predictions support decisions they may influence the outcomes they aim to predict. This work develops a risk-minimization framework for performative prediction that combines statistics, game theory, and causality. It introduces performative stability, under which predictions are calibrated against future outcomes that arise from acting on the prediction, and gives necessary and sufficient conditions for retraining to converge to a performatively stable point of nearly minimal loss.
