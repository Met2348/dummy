## Author Abstract

As LLM-based multi-agent systems are increasingly deployed for complex tasks, ensuring their reliability has become a pressing challenge. Since these systems coordinate through unstructured natural language, they are prone to semantic failures that propagate silently. We propose MAS-FIRE, a systematic framework for fault injection and reliability evaluation. It defines 15 fault types covering intra-agent cognitive errors and inter-agent coordination failures and injects them through prompt modification, response rewriting, and message-routing manipulation. Applied to three representative architectures, MAS-FIRE reveals a tiered set of fault-tolerant behaviors and shows that stronger foundation models do not uniformly improve robustness, while iterative closed-loop designs neutralize over 40 percent of faults that cause catastrophic collapse in linear workflows.
