# Collision Matrix: Ideas That Are No Longer Enough

This file is deliberately blunt. It prevents the sprint from drifting back to a
pleasant but already occupied project.

## Full-corpus addendum (2026-07-10)

The 1,150-paper audit found three decision-changing collisions that the first
screen missed:

- SEA directly implements anytime-valid gates for self-evolving harness edits.
- HARBOR directly formulates cost-aware, multi-fidelity Bayesian optimization
  over harness configurations.
- MAS-FIRE, Claw-Eval, and RepoMirage substantially crowd generic corruption and
  fault-injection benchmark claims.

SEAGym also reports cross-model sign reversals for evolved whole harnesses. A
transport paper now needs atomic interventions and prospective prediction, not
another descriptive matrix.

| Tempting first idea | Nearest collision | Why it is below the current novelty bar | What would rescue it |
|---|---|---|---|
| `3 models x 3 harnesses`, report ANOVA and ranking flips | Stop Comparing; Harness-Bench; General Agent Evaluation | model-harness variance and ranking reversal are already direct contributions | estimate transport, sequential effects, or a new population-level estimand |
| Full factorial over planning/tool/memory/reflection, then Shapley values | Cross-Component Interference; AgentSpec | exact `2^5` study, Shapley, non-submodularity, and task/scale interactions already exist | time-varying interventions or a sample-efficient selection algorithm with guarantees |
| Build a stronger self-improving harness | Meta-Harness; AHE; Self-Harness; TTHE; RHO | crowded systems race with substantial engineering and compute | study statistical validity, forward generalization, or failure conditions of evolution |
| Use RL to decide when the harness should verify | Offline Harness Control; proactive memory; AdaCoM | Harness MDP and offline RL controller appeared in July 2026 | identify causal excursion effects, not only optimize a policy |
| Counterfactually replay a failed step to assign blame | Causal Agent Replay; AgenTracer | intervention algebra, Monte Carlo Shapley, and confidence intervals already exist | solve direct-effect coupling or switch to prospective randomization |
| Compare harnesses at equal token cost | Effective Feedback Compute; equal-token SAS vs MAS; AI Agents That Matter | matched-budget design and “quality, not raw compute” are occupied | connect budget to transport or anytime-valid selection |
| Decompose model and scaffold ability with IRT | Agent Psychometrics | LLM/scaffold ability decomposition and unseen-combination prediction already exist | model non-invariance, causal transport, or adaptive evaluation |
| Add one more context/memory/tool module | AdaCoM; CWL; proactive memory; CMI; CMTF | component modules are moving too quickly and need large baselines | use the component as a controlled treatment in a broader scientific question |

## Surviving contribution slots

Two strong independent slots survive the full-corpus collision search:

1. prospective, predictive transport of atomic harness effects across sealed
   model-task cells;
2. micro-randomized sequential harness interventions.

Three original slots survive only as narrower supporting claims: empirical audits
of endogenous anytime-valid gates, cross-layer severity laws rather than another
corruption suite, and transfer-aware warm starts folded into the transport paper.
