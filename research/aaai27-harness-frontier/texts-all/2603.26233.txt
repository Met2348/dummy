F   Computing Costs
Table 5 details the total inference costs for each
evaluation setting across both LLM backbones. For
Claude Sonnet 4.5, all agent inference calls were
executed via the Anthropic API. While the multi-
agent scaffold more than doubles the total inference
cost compared to the single-agent baselines, the ab-
solute financial cost per task remains negligible.
We argue that this increase in compute expendi-
ture is a highly favorable trade-off; by proactively
resolving underspecification and significantly in-
creasing the overall task resolve rate, the multi-
agent setup ultimately saves substantial human de-
veloper time and effort that would otherwise be
spent on debugging solutions that are misaligned
with the original intent. For Kimi K2.6, all agent
inference calls were executed via the Moonshot
API. Kimi K2.6 runs at roughly 30–50% of the
cost of Claude Sonnet 4.5, offering substantial cost
efficiency gains while maintaining competitive per-
formance. Additionally, the multi-agent scaffold
has a total inference cost less than double that of
the single-agent baselines, making Kimi K2.6 a
cost-efficient alternative for deploying the frame-
