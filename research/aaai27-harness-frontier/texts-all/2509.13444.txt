B.2     Questionnaire
This appendix presents the three questionnaires employed in our study to evaluate the user experience. All items were rated on a 5-point
Likert scale, ranging from 1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree). The questionnaires are designed to assess Task Satisfaction, Interface
Satisfaction, and AI Satisfaction, respectively.
Task Satisfaction Questionnaire (TSQ).
   (1) I perceive the system as highly efficient for accomplishing my task.
   (2) I consider this system a better alternative to my usual method for completing this task.
   (3) I believe the system is flexible enough to be adapted for other related tasks.
   (4) I feel the system adapted well to my personal preferences and habits during the task.
   (5) The system provided sufficient and helpful information, considering many details on my behalf.
Interface Satisfaction Questionnaire (ISQ).
   (1) I perceive the user interface as novel.
CHI ’26, April 13–17, 2026, Barcelona, Spain                                                                                                  Xu et al.


   (2) The design of the interface is well-aligned with the task workflow.
   (3) The interface provides a strong sense of control; I clearly understood the outcome of my interactions.
   (4) I feel that the interface is tailored to my personal preferences.
   (5) I believe the user interface is easy to use.
AI Satisfaction Questionnaire (ASQ).
   (1) I was clearly aware of the AI’s assistance while using the system.
   (2) It was clear to me how to interact with the AI.
   (3) I have a clear understanding of the AI’s capabilities and limitations.
   (4) I felt a sense of close collaboration with the AI while completing the task.
   (5) I found the AI’s behavior to be predictable; I was not concerned about it performing unexpected actions.
   (6) I found communicating with the AI to be effortless.
