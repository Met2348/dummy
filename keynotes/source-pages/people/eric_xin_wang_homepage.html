About

Xin (Eric) Wang is an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science Department at UC Santa Barbara and the Head of Research at Simular. He also directs the UCSB Center for Responsible Machine Learning (CRML). His research interests include Natural Language Processing, Computer Vision, and Machine Learning, with an emphasis on Multimodal and Agentic AI. Previously, he was a faculty at UC Santa Cruz and worked at Google Research, Meta FAIR, Microsoft Research, and Adobe Research.

Eric has served as (Senior) Area Chair for conferences such as ACL, NAACL, EMNLP, ICLR, NeurIPS, and ICML, and organized workshops and tutorials at those venues. He has received several awards and recognitions for his work, including multiple best paper awards, Google Research Faculty Award, Amazon Alexa Prize Awards, JPMorganChase Faculty Research Award, Cisco Faculty Research Awards, eBay Faculty Research Awards, AAII Interdisciplinary Research Award, and various gift awards/grants from Adobe, Apple, Amazon, Snap, Microsoft, OpenAI, Cybever, Orby, etc. His research has been widely covered by outlets including VentureBeat, MIT Technology Review, and HuggingFace Daily Papers.
