\begin{abstract}
World models allocate a fixed imagination budget regardless of decision difficulty.
We propose a controller that adaptively decides when to imagine, how many candidate
futures to roll out, and when to stop trusting the rollout. In our previous work,
we showed that naively increasing rollout depth does not improve decision quality
when the imagined trajectories share the same information as the baseline critic.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Code and pretrained checkpoints are available at
\url{https://github.com/epeng-riverside-lab/imagination-budget-controller}.

\section{Method}
We formalize the decision as a value-of-computation problem over three gates:
whether to imagine, whether to trust the rollout, and when to stop.
