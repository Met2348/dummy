\begin{abstract}
World models allocate a fixed imagination budget regardless of decision difficulty.
We propose a controller that adaptively decides when to imagine, how many candidate
futures to roll out, and when to stop trusting the rollout. Peng \& Lin (2025)
previously showed that naively increasing rollout depth does not improve decision
quality when the imagined trajectories share the same information as the baseline
critic.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Code and pretrained checkpoints are available at an anonymized repository:
\url{https://anonymous.4open.science/r/imagination-budget-controller-4F21}.

\section{Method}
We formalize the decision as a value-of-computation problem over three gates:
whether to imagine, whether to trust the rollout, and when to stop.
